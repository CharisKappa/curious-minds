Money,
Decoded.

From the cotton paper in your pocket to the global financial system it represents — and how to put your share of it to work, instead of watching it quietly disappear.

Four chapters · ~3 hours reading · Built for the curious

This is a guide written for the person who suspects they should understand money better but hasn't found a way in. Not a finance degree. Not a sales pitch for an investment app. Not a get-rich-quick scheme. Just a clear, opinionated walk through what money is, how the economy works, what you can do with the money you have, and the specific traps that catch almost everyone who tries.

It assumes nothing. It builds up. By the time you finish Chapter 4, you'll have a coherent mental model of the system you live inside — and a concrete framework for participating in it on better terms than 90% of people.

The chapters build on each other. Reading them in order is strongly recommended, because the answers in later chapters only make sense once you've absorbed the questions in earlier ones. Why does inflation exist? Why do central banks want some of it? Why is "leaving money in the bank" a slow loss? Why do most professional fund managers fail to beat the market? Why does a piece of paper have value at all? All of it connects.

A confession before we start

Nothing in this guide is personalized financial advice. The author isn't your advisor, doesn't know your situation, and isn't paid by anyone selling you anything. Treat it as a map for understanding the territory — not a route. Before deploying real money, especially in significant amounts, talk to a fee-only financial professional (one who charges you directly, rather than earning commissions on products they sell). What this guide gives you is the vocabulary, the framework, and the skepticism to have that conversation well.
